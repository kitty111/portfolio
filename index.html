Ectrocardiogram analysis website (EcgApp)

This four-month internship at IRD's UMMISCO laboratory aims to add value to the researchers' work by providing an application platform for exploiting AI algorithms in the context of electrocardiogram (ECG) biosignals.

More specifically, the detailed objectives of the web platform are defined as follows:

- Ergonomic human-machine interface: create an intuitive (user-friendly), ergonomic and easy-to-use interface that simplifies use of the platform. Users, whether clinicians, researchers or engineers, must be able to use the platform without technical barriers.

- Automatic ECG digitization and parsing (conversion): enable automatic conversion of paper ECGs (PDF and image) into digital signals usable by Deep Learning algorithms.

- Automatic analysis of ECG signals: enable Deep Learning algorithms to detect and prevent cardiac pathologies by analyzing ECG signals.

- Indexing a massive central ECG database: the laboratory has set up a massive ECG database cluster hosting millions of signals from hospitals, clinics, institutes and partners across different countries. The aim here is to (i) provide an optimal search engine for rapidly indexing and searching patient data in this database, (ii) provide a module for loading and visualizing data and (interactive) ECGs

In addition to these operational goals, we have also implemented a Continuous Integration and Delivery (CI/CD) system for our lab team to test projects, provide feedback and track progress.

Job recommendation system

The project aimed to create a sophisticated job offer recommendation system along with a student profiling system. This initiative was recognized as the best Data Science (DS) project at the Esprit 2022 event.

The job offer recommendation system aimed to assist students in finding professional opportunities that matched their skills, interests, and aspirations. It relied on advanced machine learning algorithms to analyze student profiles and available job offers, generating personalized recommendations.

The student profiling system, on the other hand, enabled the collection and organization of information about students, including their skills, academic and professional experiences, and areas of interest.
